About me

Yuan Sun is a Ph.D. student in the Rinker School of Construction Management at the University of Florida. She will receive her Ph.D. by August, 2024.

Her research interest focuses on investigating the implication of human-technology interactions within construction domain. Specifically, she is interested in exploring the applications of emerging technologies to promote construction education and training.

Under this big picture of her research interest, she has been conducting research in two areas of (1) Enhancing learning experience for current students and workers in construction and (2) Investigating safety concerns of human-robot collaboration. Her research efforts follow multidisciplinary study methodologies that blend human-technology interaction and educational technology. She has the experience and willingness to engage in collaboration with construction industry practitioners and experts from various areas (such as education and computer science).

Yuan sees teaching as an integral part of academic activities. After research findings are established, teaching provides a natural path for the transfer of knowledge. New cutting-edge technologies are becoming more important than ever in the construction industry. She believes that her role as an instructor is to enable students to become independent lifelong learners and prepare them for the future of work by bringing applied cutting-edge research and knowledge from real-world construction work contexts to her classroom. She as teaching assistant is supporting Building Information Modeling and Digital Twins for the Built Environment at University of Florida.
